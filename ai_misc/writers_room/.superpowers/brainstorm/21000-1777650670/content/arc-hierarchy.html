Arc Hierarchy

Three levels — Project story arc → Character arc → Scene arc advancement

The Flow

Level 1 — Project The overall story arc of the entire production
project.story_arc
project.plot_points (json)
The macro narrative — what the entire production is about from beginning to end. Defines the journey all characters collectively take. Example: "A reckless teen learns that actions have consequences — past, present, and future."
↓ each character derives their arc from the story arc ↓
Level 2 — Character Arc Each character's personal journey within this production — derived from the story arc
character.character_arc
(on Character model)

character_arcs table:
character_id
project_id
arc_description
arc_start_state
arc_end_goal
current_position

character.character_arc — the character's timeless arc concept (who they are across all productions).
Example: "Marty's arc: from reckless risk-taker to someone who understands consequences."

character_arcs (join) — how that arc manifests in this specific project, where they start and where they need to end up.
In BTTF1: starts overconfident, must learn humility. In BTTF2: starts cautious, tempted back into recklessness.

↓ each scene advances the character along their arc ↓
Level 3 — Scene Arc Advancement Each scene moves the character forward along their arc
scene_characters table:
scene_id
character_id
scene_objectives
arc_advancement
(how this scene
moves the arc)
Each scene appearance records what arc movement happens here. Collectively, scene arc advancements move the character from arc_start_state → arc_end_goal, which in turn moves the project's story_arc forward.

Scene 1: Marty is cocky and dismissive. Scene 4: first crack of doubt. Scene 8: fully transformed.

How This Changes the System Prompt

Layer 2 — Character (updated)

personality, voice_pattern
+ character.character_arc (timeless arc concept)
+ character_arc.arc_start_state (where they begin in this project)
+ character_arc.current_position (where they are NOW)

Layer 3 — Project (updated)

title, description, genre, setting, tone
+ project.story_arc (the macro narrative)
+ project.plot_points (key story beats)

Layer 4 — Scene (updated)

context, beat_structure, cast list
+ scene_character.scene_objectives
+ scene_character.arc_advancement (what arc movement this scene delivers)

After the scene run

character_arc.current_position is updated to reflect the arc advancement from this scene — the character carries their new position into the next scene.
